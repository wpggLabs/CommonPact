# Abuse and Moderation

Open coordination creates abuse channels as well as freedom from platform lock-in.

## Abuse classes

- spam and discovery flooding;
- stalking, location correlation, and coercive contact;
- fraudulent identity, credentials, listings, or evidence;
- colluding reputation and receipt farms;
- harassment, discrimination, threats, and retaliation;
- prohibited goods or services;
- malicious clients hiding terms or fees;
- relay censorship or selective delivery;
- fraudulent payment, escrow, refund, or donation behavior;
- governance capture and false certification.

## Layered controls

The core provides signed provenance, expiry, revocation, warnings, causal references, and portable evidence. Profiles define prohibited public data, domain warnings, signer requirements, and high-risk behavior. Clients and communities apply local filtering, block lists, rate limits, reporting, support, and sanctions. Operators and authorities remain responsible for real-world intervention.

## No universal moderation authority

CommonPact does not define one global ban list or truth authority. Communities may share signed warnings and policies. Clients MUST display provenance and SHOULD distinguish allegation, verified credential status, protocol evidence, and external adjudication.

## Appeal and error

A warning must be scoped, attributable, revocable where appropriate, and distinguishable from a legal finding. Deployments SHOULD publish appeal, correction, retention, and emergency escalation processes.
