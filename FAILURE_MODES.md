# Failure Modes

## Protocol failures

- clients calculate different event IDs;
- profiles disagree on signers;
- reordered events create inconsistent states;
- extensions silently change agreement meaning;
- export loses provenance;
- version negotiation downgrades security.

## Product failures

- keys are lost;
- interfaces hide fees or terms;
- notifications fail;
- mobile background delivery is unreliable;
- ranking favors sponsors without disclosure;
- optional providers become practical monopolies.

## Social and market failures

- no counterparties exist;
- fraud and spam overwhelm users;
- communities fragment;
- trust requirements exclude legitimate users;
- reputation farming creates false confidence;
- branding is used to imply safety.

## Domain failures

- Ride: unsafe pickup, uninsured operation, emergency gaps.
- Rental: theft, damage disagreement, unlawful deposit handling.
- Delivery: custody loss, prohibited goods, recipient privacy leaks.
- Fund: fraudulent campaign, sanctions, tax, charity, custody, or refund failure.

## Governance failures

The founder becomes permanent gatekeeper, a sponsor controls the roadmap, registry inclusion is mistaken for permission, objections are ignored, or documentation overstates implementation.
