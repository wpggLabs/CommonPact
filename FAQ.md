# Frequently Asked Questions

## Is CommonPact an application?
No. It is a protocol proposal and profile framework.

## Is it one marketplace for rides, rentals, delivery, and donations?
No. A multi-profile client is possible, but one super-app is not required.

## Does it eliminate all middlemen?
No. It removes the mandatory platform owner at the protocol layer. Payment, insurance, identity, moderation, support, escrow, and dispute providers may remain useful or required.

## Is it peer-to-peer?
The agreement is directly between parties and no central platform is authoritative. Delivery may still use internet relays or servers.

## Is it a Bluetooth mesh?
No. Nearby networking may help verification and outages. Citywide discovery should initially use replaceable internet infrastructure.

## Is it like email?
Conceptually: different clients and providers interoperate through shared standards. CommonPact must also learn from email's spam, metadata, and concentration problems.

## Does CommonPact charge a commission?
No protocol commission is required.

## Can applications charge?
Yes, for transparent optional services.

## Is PactRide dependent on CommonPact?
No. PactRide remains self-contained. Alignment requires an adapter or explicit future version.

## Are the other profiles finished?
PactRental is a detailed draft. PactDelivery and PactFund are candidate concepts.

## Is CommonPact production-ready?
No. It is a pre-implementation research RFC.
