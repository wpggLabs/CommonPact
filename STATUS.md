# CommonPact Project Status

**Last reviewed:** 2026-07-11

## Founder-vision completion

The complete initial founder vision is represented publicly. The repository now documents the social objective, protocol architecture, precise core semantics, event validation, profile standard, PactRide mapping, a full PactRental documentation package, user journeys, adoption strategy, accessibility, abuse, key recovery, portability, legal boundaries, economics, governance, schemas, cryptographic fixtures, automation, and the project website.

This completion is bounded: it means the founder no longer needs private chat history to explain the intended system. It does not mean the protocol is correct, independently implemented, production-safe, legally approved, funded, adopted, or operating.

## What exists

- Complete vision, problem, principles, non-goals, glossary, journeys, and adoption model.
- Normative draft v0.1 core with deterministic validation order and error codes.
- Strict envelope, profile-manifest, event-payload, and PactRental schemas.
- Cryptographically complete Ed25519 core fixtures plus semantic invalid cases.
- Generic lifecycle, evidence, transport, identity, extension, and portability rules.
- A complete profile documentation standard.
- PactRide mapping without a runtime dependency.
- PactRental as a complete founder-authored Research RFC profile package and abstraction test.
- PactDelivery and PactFund concept boundaries and public research issues.
- Detailed privacy, security, abuse, accessibility, legal, payment, economics, governance, and maintenance boundaries.
- Automated artifact and claim-alignment checks.
- A purpose-built GitHub Pages site and documented visual system.

## What remains unproven

- No production CommonPact client or relay network.
- No two independent CommonPact implementations.
- No independently reproduced cross-language report.
- No real profile registry or certification program.
- No external security or legal review.
- No operational proof for PactRental, PactDelivery, or PactFund.
- No accountable payment, escrow, insurance, identity, moderation, or dispute deployment.
- No funded implementation team or committed pilot.
- No claim that CommonPact removes every intermediary or makes any domain safe or lawful.

## Immediate evidence gates

1. Obtain substantive independent review.
2. Implement core validation in at least two languages.
3. Independently reproduce all cryptographic fixtures.
4. Demonstrate two independently written clients exchanging and importing one minimal pact.
5. Implement the PactRental transcript and document abstraction failures.
6. Define and measure one internet transport profile.
7. Model metadata leakage, Sybil behavior, evidence farming, and practical recentralization.
8. Obtain domain review before PactDelivery or PactFund advances.
9. Recruit at least one sustained technical maintainer or institutional partner.
10. Obtain legal and operational review before a real pilot.

## Claim discipline

CommonPact may be described as an open, profile-based, pre-implementation research RFC with a complete documented founder vision and initial machine-readable artifacts.

It must not be described as a launched marketplace, production network, verified trust system, guaranteed zero-fee service, operating donation system, substitute for accountable operators, or independently proven standard.
