# Trust and Reputation

CommonPact does not define a universal reputation number.

Clients may evaluate completed receipts, distinct counterparties, credentials, key history, warnings, revocations, ratings, community membership, profile qualifications, and local policy.

Every signal should retain issuer, subject, scope, issue time, expiry, revocation status, referenced pact, and verification method.

## Sybil and farming risk

Colluding actors can create keys, fake interactions, and reciprocal ratings. Signed receipts alone do not prevent farming. Research must test repeated-pair detection, graph diversity, credential cost, community attestations, rate limits, privacy-preserving continuity, and false positives against legitimate repeat relationships.

Different communities may accept different evidence. Clients should make policy visible and must not present local acceptance as universal truth.
