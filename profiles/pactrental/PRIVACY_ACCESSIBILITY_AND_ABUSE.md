# PactRental Privacy, Accessibility, and Abuse

## Sensitive data

Exact storage location, keys and access codes, serial numbers, identity documents, ownership documents, travel schedules, security systems, full condition media, and payment credentials must remain private by default.

## Metadata risks

Repeated availability can reveal valuable assets, empty properties, routines, wealth, and travel. Clients should rotate discovery identities, coarsen location, limit history, and avoid broadcasting exact asset identifiers.

## Accessibility

Terms may describe delivery or accessible handoff, communication method, additional instruction time, adaptive controls, mobility constraints, and assistance needs without requiring public diagnosis disclosure.

## Abuse cases

- fake ownership or duplicate listings;
- theft planning through discovery metadata;
- coerced handoff;
- hidden terms or damage policies;
- fabricated condition evidence;
- abusive tracking devices;
- discriminatory credential or deposit requirements;
- colluding receipts and ratings;
- retaliatory warnings.

Protocol evidence helps attribution but does not replace inspection, insurance, support, emergency response, or law enforcement.
